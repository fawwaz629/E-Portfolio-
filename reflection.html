Malaysia's leading cybersecurity firm. Penetration testing, digital forensics, red teaming, security consulting.
Why do you want to work for LGMS?
LGMS is the organisation in Malaysia that conducts the most technically rigorous cybersecurity work — penetration testing, digital forensics, red team engagements — for high-stakes clients including financial institutions and government agencies. My degree is in Computer Network Security, and I am seeking an environment that demands and develops that specialisation. LGMS offers proximity to senior practitioners, exposure to the full range of security disciplines, and meaningful contribution to Malaysia's digital security infrastructure from the earliest stage of a career.
What value can you bring?
I am able to design, build, and deploy machine learning-based security tooling — a capability uncommon at fresh graduate level. PhishShield is a deployed Flask application serving a trained Random Forest classifier, with a live Chrome extension, built without external guidance. Beyond machine learning, my breadth across Python, Java, SQL, and web technologies means I can contribute across the development lifecycle.
Why should we hire you?
My CGPA is 2.82. I acknowledge that this falls below the threshold some organisations apply at the screening stage. What I offer in its place is a working, independently developed security application. PhishShield progressed from raw dataset through feature engineering, model training, Flask API deployment, and browser extension integration. The code is publicly available. I would ask that the evidence of what I have actually built be considered alongside the academic transcript.
What skills make you suitable?
Python with Scikit-learn and Flask for machine learning deployment; full-stack development across Java, SQL, HTML, and CSS; Chrome Extension development; understanding of phishing attack mechanics, URL feature extraction, and classifier evaluation. Bilingual in Bahasa Melayu and English — a practical asset for a Malaysian firm working with government and enterprise clients.
Why would a company choose to hire you? — the employer's perspective
Three attributes that reduce hiring risk: first, a working security product delivered independently — shorter gap between hiring and productive contribution. Second, technical breadth across Python, Java, SQL, and web development means I can be deployed across the lifecycle. Third, professional experience in demanding service environments — hospitality and event management — has built discipline, composure, and communication skills that are difficult to develop in an academic setting alone.
What are your strengths and weaknesses?
Strengths: deployed a working security application independently, proficient in Python and Flask in an applied context, three completed projects across degree and diploma, strong personal values, bilingual. Weaknesses: no formal security certifications yet, CGPA of 2.82 below some screening thresholds, limited professional IT experience, practical networking tools at an early stage.
